On August 15 1988, a song played when Rajiv Gandhi finished his Independence Day's speech. [*Mile Sur Mera Tumahara*](https://youtu.be/k8DtPqXjptM) - the song of national integration played after the Prime Minister's speech. It would come to define the theme of national integration after being constantly replayed in the media starved India of late 80's and 90's. 

The 80's had been tumultous decade for India. Operation Bluestar, Indira Gandhi's killing, Sikh massacre that followed, Shah Bano Case and Babri Masjid Case had sparks of communalism. 80's was also the time when India tried to break free of License Raj era and embrace technology for a forward looking nation. The need for the feeling of unity behind the diversity was needed.

Piyush Pandey of Oglivy and Mather wrote the lyrics for song. Pandit Bhimsen Joshi composed it in Raag Bhairavi. This was the first the mainstream television media captured the diversity of India in a way that has not been done previously. It started with Bhimsen Joshi in his signature husky voice singing behind the iconic Liril Falls of Kodaikanal. The translation of *Mile Sur Mera Tumahara* is sung in all languages mentioned in 8th Schedule of constitution. The regional diversity is captured through farming family of Punjab, mountains of Kashmir, elephants of Kerala, Chennai's Marina Beach, a Kodava family, Kolkata Metro and Mario Miranda painting in Goa. The jingle ends with children marching down dressed in colours of Indian flag. The jingle explicitly avoided religion identities in the representations of India. 

This jingle can be critiqued in a many ways. Primary among them would be that it has overwhelmingly large parts in Hindi and not enough regional representation. The attempts to re-make a second *Mile Sur Mera Tumahara* will be impossible in today's time given how polarised today's India is on regional lines. The 2011 attempt was a caricature at its best of the original version. The recreation will need Piyush Pandey and Bhimsen Joshi of present times to imagine national integration in the contemporary times.
